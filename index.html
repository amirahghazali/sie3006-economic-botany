Davalia sp

  • Common Name: Rabbit’s foot fern / Squirrel’s foot fern
  • Scientific Name: Davallia sp.
  • Family: Davalliaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Epiphytic or lithophytic fern with creeping, hairy rhizomes that resemble a rabbit’s foot; covered in brown or silvery hairs.
  • Habitat:
    1. Grows on tree trunks, exposed roots, rocks, and shaded forest surfaces.
    2. Prefers humid, shaded tropical and subtropical environments.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Rhizomes occasionally used in traditional remedies.
    2. Widely used as an ornamental plant due to its unique hairy rhizomes and elegant fronds.

  • Taxonomic Description:

    2. Found on tree trunks, fallen logs, or moist ground.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Rhizomes and fronds used in traditional medicine for anti-inflammatory and cooling remedies.
    2. Also used ornamentally as a decorative fern.

Selaginella sp

  • Common Name: Spike moss, Lesser clubmoss
  • Scientific Name: Selaginella sp
  • Family: Selaginellaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Small, creeping or ascending herbaceous plant forming dense mats.
    2. Slender, dichotomously branched stems.
    3. Leaves (microphylls) small (2–5 mm), scale-like, arranged in four rows, single-veined.
  • Habitat:
    1. Moist, shaded areas, forest floors, rocky damp surfaces, near streams, rotting logs in tropical forests.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Whole plant used traditionally for anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
    2. Leaves/stems commonly used in herbal remedies.
×

Dicranopteris linearis

  • Common Name: Old World forked fern / Fork fern
  • Scientific Name: Dicranopteris linearis
  • Family: Gleicheniaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Terrestrial fern forming dense thickets with long, scrambling or climbing fronds.
    2. Fronds fork repeatedly (dichotomous branching), giving a distinctive “Y-shaped” pattern.
    3. Stipes slender and wiry.
    4. Pinnae are narrow, linear, and arranged along each branch.
  • Habitat:
    1. Common in disturbed areas, open forests, grasslands, roadsides, and hill slopes.
    2. Prefers sunny to partially shaded environments with well-drained, acidic soils.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Young shoots sometimes consumed as traditional vegetables in certain regions.
    2. Whole plant used in folk medicine for cooling, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing purposes.
    3. Helps in soil stabilization on slopes due to dense growth.
×

Lycopodiella sp

  • Common Name: Clubmoss / Bog clubmoss
  • Scientific Name: Lycopodiella sp
  • Family: Lycopodiaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Small, terrestrial, creeping herb forming low mats.
    2. Stems slender, trailing, with upright fertile shoots.
  • Habitat:
    1. Moist, open areas such as forest edges, peatlands, swampy grounds, and wet sandy soils.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Whole plant sometimes used in traditional remedies.
    2. Spores from cones used historically for drying powders and ceremonial uses.
×

Blechnum sp

  • Common Name: Hard fern / Deer fern
  • Scientific Name: Blechnum sp
  • Family: Blechnaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Terrestrial fern with erect or creeping rhizomes.
    2. Fronds dimorphic:
      1. Sterile fronds: broader, pinnate, bright green.
      2. Fertile fronds: narrower with tightly packed pinnae bearing sori.
  • Habitat:
    1. Moist, shaded forest floors; prefers humid tropical or montane environments, near streams or damp soils.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Young fronds are sometimes consumed traditionally (edible shoots in some species).
    2. Rhizomes/fronds occasionally used in traditional remedies.
    3. Ornamental value in landscaping.
×

Aglaomorpha sp

  • Common Name: Bear’s paw fern / Basket fern
  • Scientific Name: Aglaomorpha sp.
  • Family: Polypodiaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Large epiphytic or terrestrial fern with thick, creeping rhizomes covered in dense brown scales.
    2. Fronds dimorphic:
      1. Sterile fronds: broad, usually forming a “basket” shape that traps debris.
  • Habitat:
    1. Common in humid lowland and montane tropical forests.
    2. Grows on tree trunks, rocks, or occasionally on the forest floor.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Rhizomes and fronds used in traditional medicine (anti-inflammatory, wound care).
    2. Popular as an ornamental fern due to its large, attractive foliage.
×

Lygodium sp

  • Common Name: Climbing fern / Old World climbing fern
  • Scientific Name: Lygodium sp.
  • Family: Lygodiaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Fern with long, climbing or twining fronds.
    2. Fronds pinnate and flexible, can grow very long
    3. Stipes slender; leaflets (pinnae) small and arranged along the rachis.
  • Habitat:
    1. Found in forests, open areas, and disturbed sites.
    2. Prefers sunny to partially shaded areas with moist soils.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Young fronds sometimes used in traditional medicine.
    2. Whole plant used for decorative purposes.
    3. Can help cover bare soil and prevent erosion.
×

Pyrrosia sp

  • Common Name: Felt fern / Leatherleaf fern
  • Scientific Name: Pyrrosia sp.
  • Family: Polypodiaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Small to medium-sized epiphytic fern.
    2. Fronds simple, leathery, sometimes covered in fine hairs.
    3. Stipes short; fronds can be oblong or lance-shaped.
  • Habitat:
    1. Grows on tree trunks, rocks, or walls in shaded, humid forests.
    2. Prefers moist, shaded tropical environments.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Fronds used in traditional medicine for cooling and wound healing.
    2. Popular as an ornamental plant due to hardy, decorative leaves.
×

Adiantum sp

  • Common Name: Maidenhair fern
  • Scientific Name: Adiantum sp.
  • Family: Pteridaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Small to medium-sized fern with delicate, fan-shaped fronds.
    2. Fronds pinnate with black, wiry stipes.
    3. Leaflets (pinnae) smooth, thin, and light green.
  • Habitat:
    1. Grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors, stream banks, and rocky crevices.
    2. Prefers humid, tropical environments.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Fronds used in traditional medicine for cooling and respiratory remedies.
    2. Popular ornamental fern due to delicate, attractive foliage.
×

Nephrolepis sp

  • Common Name: Sword fern / Boston fern
  • Scientific Name: Nephrolepis sp.
  • Family: Nephrolepidaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Medium to large fern with long, arching fronds.
    2. Fronds pinnate, bright green, and leathery.
    3. Stipes short to medium, slightly hairy; pinnae narrow and arranged along the rachis.
  • Habitat:
    1. Found on forest floors, shaded areas, and sometimes as epiphytes.
    2. Prefers humid, tropical, and partially shaded environments.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Fronds used in traditional medicine and ornamental planting.
    2. Helps improve air quality when grown indoors.
×

Huperzia sp

  • Common Name: Firmoss / Clubmoss
  • Scientific Name: Huperzia sp.
  • Family: Lycopodiaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Small, creeping or erect fern-like plant.
    2. Stems slender, branched dichotomously.
    3. Leaves (microphylls) small, needle-like, spirally arranged along stems.
    4. Produces small strobili (cones) at stem tips.
  • Habitat:
    1. Found in moist, shaded forests, often on soil, rocks, or tree bases.
    2. Prefers cool, humid tropical or subtropical environments.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Whole plant used in traditional medicine for anti-inflammatory and wound-healing purposes.
    2. Spores sometimes used historically for ceremonial or medicinal purposes.
×

Platycerium sp

  • Common Name: Staghorn fern
  • Scientific Name:Platycerium sp.
  • Family: Polypodiaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Large epiphytic fern with two types, Sterile fronds and Fertile fronds.
    2. Fronds leathery, green to grayish-green.
    3. Rhizomes short, anchoring the plant to tree trunks or rocks.
  • Habitat:
    1. Found on tree trunks, rocks, and occasionally on forest floors.
    2. Prefers humid, shaded tropical forests.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Fronds used ornamentally for decoration and landscaping.
    2. Some traditional medicinal uses in certain cultures.
×

Asplenium nidus

  • Common Name: Bird’s nest fern
  • Scientific Name: Asplenium nidus
  • Family: Aspleniaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Large, epiphytic fern forming a rosette of broad, simple fronds.
    2. Fronds strap-shaped, bright green, wavy margins.
    3. Central “nest” formed by tightly clustered young fronds.
    4. Rhizomes short, thick, and creeping, anchoring the plant to tree trunks.
  • Habitat:
    1. Found on tree trunks, rocks, or occasionally on forest floors.
    2. Prefers humid, shaded tropical forests.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Fronds used ornamentally for decoration.
    2. Traditionally used in medicine for cooling and wound-healing properties.
×

Parablechnum minus

  • Common Name: Soft water fern / Blechnum fern
  • Scientific Name: Parablechnum minus
  • Family: Blechnaceae
  • Taxonomic Desription:
    1. Medium-sized terrestrial fern with upright fronds.
    2. Fronds pinnate, soft, and light to dark green.
    3. Fertile fronds are narrower with rows of sori on the underside.
    4. Rhizomes short and creeping.
  • Habitat:
    1. Found in moist, shaded forest floors and along streams.
    2. Prefers humid, cool environments with damp soil.
  • Purpose / Beneficial use:
    1. Young fronds sometimes used in traditional remedies.
    2. Plant helps maintain moisture and ground cover in forest ecosystems.
